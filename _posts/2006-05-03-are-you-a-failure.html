An interesting post on
why failing is good. I can think of a few times when I've failed and I learnt from
these. One being to take my notes to open book exams, that really helps, and the other
is to not drink too much alchohol the night before an exam. But besides from the comical
failures I've suffered I've worked on projects where things havn't gone according
to plan, and not worked out how I had hoped. I've always tried to not let these happen
to me again in later projects and changed how I approach certain problems. Failing
is always good, you do learn alot more from seeing what you can't do than what
you can do.

So is failing to fail counted as a failure?
